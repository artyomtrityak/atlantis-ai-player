# The Covenant of Shadows — Flexible Strategy

**Faction 4 | Playstyle: Summoning + Combat Magic | Economy: Minimal, magic-support only**

---

## 1. Strategic Pillars

1. **Magic First, Always** — Every silver decision passes through "does this enable magic development?" Economy exists only to pay mage upkeep and study costs. Never let study stop due to silver shortage.

2. **Never Expose a Mage Unprotected** — Seraphel and all future mages stay BEHIND + AVOID in every region. A dead mage loses months of invested silver and study. Until a combat guard exists, do not move mages through uncontrolled hexes.

3. **Parallel Fire and Necromancy from the Start** — Seraphel pursues FIRE 2 in parallel with NECR path. Fire provides combat power now; necromancy provides the army later. Neither branch waits for the other.

4. **Scouts Before Seraphel Moves** — Buy cheap goblin or local-race scouts to map adjacents before committing Seraphel. A random bad hex (hostile faction, monster lair) destroys the faction if the only mage dies in transit.

5. **Faction Upgrade Points Are Never Wasted** — FACTION MAGIC and MARTIAL upgrade silver is spent every single turn. Magic cap = #1 priority. Unused upgrade points are permanently lost income.

6. **Build Toward a Self-Sustaining Summoning Engine** — Target: one dedicated summon-caster producing undead each turn, a second combat mage handling fire, Seraphel overseeing the whole. Once the engine runs, the army grows automatically.

## 2. Phase Framework

### Phase 1 — Establishment
*Trigger: Start of game (already in progress after turn 1)*

**Primary focus:** Find a safe settlement, establish minimal silver income, begin Seraphel's core magic path.

**Actions:**
- Buy 2–3 goblin scouts immediately. Send in all accessible directions. Wait 1 turn for reports before moving Seraphel.
- Target: large swamp city (population 3000+) within 2 hexes, OR the current swamp (30,8) if no better option is found quickly.
- If current location (Taso swamp 30,8) has no hostile factions and no city guard: stay, begin taxing.
- Form 1 tax guard unit (buy local goblins, study COMB, GUARD + TAX) to generate income while Seraphel studies.
- Claim unclaimed silver each turn as needed. Reserve enough to fund 2 mage studies + upkeep without touching claimed funds.
- Seraphel: STUDY NECR → SUSK (next steps) while holding FIRE 1 ready for combat.
- Upgrade faction points: FACTION MAGIC every time possible.

**Success conditions (proceed to Phase 2 when ALL met):**
- A permanent base hex chosen with no immediate threat
- At least 300–500 silver/turn income from tax + claimed funds covering 2+ turns of mage study
- Seraphel has NECR 2 OR SUSK 1 unlocked
- At least 1 mage slot available for a second mage

---

### Phase 2 — The Awakening
*Trigger: Phase 1 success conditions met*

**Primary focus:** Recruit second mage, unlock skeleton summoning, establish Fire 2 on Seraphel, build the summoning engine.

**Actions:**
- Buy a second leader at the nearest city. This is "Mage 2" — dedicated to the Fire + summoning support path.
- Seraphel teaches prerequisites to Mage 2 (force, spirit) while continuing own study.
- Seraphel studies SUSK 1 (requires NECR 1 already held — unlock immediately once NECR 1 confirmed).
- Begin casting CAST SUMMON_SKELETONS each turn once SUSK unlocked. Build skeleton pool.
- Seraphel simultaneously studies FIRE 2 over 2–3 turns (FIRE 1 already held; just needs the 100 silver study).
- Third mage recruited when Magic cap allows. Assign to Necromancy secondary path or DEMO (Demon Lore) for future summons.
- Form a skeleton carrier unit (1 goblin leader, cheap, just carries the undead army). Never leave skeletons stranded with the casting mage who needs mobility.
- Build a combat guard force around the mage base (10–20 goblins, COMB 1) for regional defense.
- Upgrade FACTION MAGIC to 3 once affordable. This is the single most important upgrade.

**Success conditions (proceed to Phase 3 when ALL met):**
- 15–20 skeletons in reserve (after decay)
- Seraphel has FIRE 2 and SUSK 1+
- Second mage operational and studying
- 500+ silver/turn reliable income
- Safe base in a defensible settlement

---

### Phase 3 — The Covenant Ascendant
*Trigger: Phase 2 success conditions met*

**Primary focus:** Army ready for combat, monster hunting, expanding territory.

**Actions:**
- Seraphel studies toward NECR 2 (enables RAIS — Raise Undead, higher quality undead), or DEMO for demon summoning.
- Dedicated summon-caster produces skeletons each turn. Target: 30+ skeletal warriors.
- Third mage develops advanced combat: FIRE 2 → FIRE 3 (fireball-level damage) or defensive shields (ESHI, SSHI).
- Begin hunting weaker monster lairs: ruins, bogs, small creature groups. Use fire + skeleton screen.
- Send a Farsight scout or use CAST FARS to survey monster lairs before committing the army.
- Build fort at home base for mage protection + study above level 2.
- Begin faction limit expansion to support 4+ mages.

## 4. Skill Development Priorities

**Ordered rules (apply in this order when choosing what to study):**

1. **Magic prerequisites first** — If a planned skill requires a prereq not yet met, study that prereq before anything else. Never skip.
2. **FIRE 2 before SUSK 1** — Fire doubles Seraphel's immediate combat output. Do this even if tempted to rush summoning.
3. **SUSK 1 as soon as FIRE 2 complete** — This starts the army-building loop. Every turn without SUSK is one fewer skeleton.
4. **NECR 2 before advanced demon path** — Raise Undead is far more powerful than DEMO in the long run for The Covenant's theme.
5. **FIRE is always the combat spell** — Never change Seraphel's combat spell away from FIRE.
6. **Study vs Cast rule:** Cast SUSK every turn once unlocked. If the mage can both study and cast in one month (per game rules), do both. Do NOT skip casting for extra study turns.
7. **PATT practice passively** — Every gate jump, every CAST gives PATT practice. Factor this into study choices.

## 5. Summoning Army Doctrine

### Skeleton Accumulation Rules

- **Decay rate:** Skeletons decay at approximately 8% per turn. Account for this in pool management.
- **SUSK 1:** Summons 2 skeletons/cast. SUSK 2: 4/cast. Two casters at SUSK 2 = 8 skeletons/turn before decay.
- **Minimum viable pool:** 15 skeletons before any combat deployment. Below this, the army is not tactically useful.
- **Target pool for monster hunting:** 30+ skeletons with fire mage support.
- **Target pool for city assault:** 50+ skeletons + allied support.
- **Carrier unit rule:** Always have a separate "skeleton carrier" unit (1 cheap unit) to hold the army. The summoner should not be the only keeper — if the mage moves, skeletons stay; if the skeletons need to deploy, the mage stays safe.

### Army Composition Targets

| Phase | Skeletons | Mages | Combat Goblins |
|-------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Phase 1 | 0 | 1 | 5–10 (basic guard) |
| Phase 2 | 15–20 | 2 | 10–20 |
| Phase 3 | 30–50 | 3+ | 20–30 |
| Endgame | 60+ | 4+ | 30+ with weapons |

### When to Deploy vs Keep Building

- **Deploy** when: target is a small monster lair (3–8 creatures), skeleton pool is above 20, fire mage is present for support.
- **Keep building** when: pool is below 15, hostile factions nearby, mage base is not secured.
- **Never deploy** without: a fire mage in the combat stack, skeletons at full pool, the base hex secured.
- **Combat formation:** Skeletons in FRONT (they are expendable), goblins/human guards FRONT, mages BEHIND with combat spell active.

## 6. Economic Support Rules

### Minimum Economy Targets

- **Phase 1:** 300 silver/turn (covers 1 mage study at 100/month + 50/month upkeep + 100 buffer)
- **Phase 2:** 500 silver/turn (covers 2 mage studies + upkeep + skeleton carrier maintenance)
- **Phase 3:** 800+ silver/turn (covers 3 mages + combat units + faction upgrades)

### Tax Unit Rules

- **When to form a tax unit:** As soon as Seraphel settles in a safe region. First turn of settlement = form goblin guard (5–8 goblins, COMB study, then GUARD + TAX).
- **Guard unit prerequisite:** Units MUST have COMB 1 before they can GUARD. Study combat before attempting to guard.
- **Unit count per region:** 1 guard unit per controlled region minimum. More if the region tax base is large.
- **Goblin cost:** ~$29 each in swamp. 8 goblins = $232 upfront, generates $50–300/turn depending on region. Pay-off in 1–5 turns.
- **When to expand tax:** When silver reserves drop below 500 OR when a new region is scouted empty, form a new guard unit immediately.

### Claim Rules

- Claim unclaimed silver each turn for study costs. Reserve enough for 2 turns of mage study at all times.
- After Phase 1 is established, reduce CLAIM frequency — rely on tax income to reduce dependence on the finite unclaimed pool.
- Current unclaimed: 9,750 silver. This is the survival buffer. Do not drain it below 3,000 until reliable income exceeds 400/turn.

### Faction Upgrade Order

Every turn, in priority order:
1. FACTION MAGIC (raises mage cap) — spend if available
2. FACTION MARTIAL (raises region cap) — spend if Magic cap already at target
3. Never skip a turn without checking if faction upgrade is possible

## 7. Safety and Survival Rules

### Settlement Selection Criteria (in priority order)

1. No city guard present (or city guard already defeated by allies)
2. Goblin-populated swamp preferred (cheap recruits at $29, native to faction start region)
3. Population 1000+ for viable tax base
4. Not adjacent to confirmed hostile faction territory
5. Has a Gate nearby (enables future escape or scouting jumps)

### Mage Protection Protocols

- Seraphel and all mages: always `AVOID 1`, `BEHIND 1`, `REVEAL FACTION` (diplomatic transparency)
- Never leave a mage alone in a hex without a combat guard unit present (at minimum 5 fighters at COMB 1)
- Do not move mages through unknown hexes — always scout first
- Mages studying: keep them stationary in the settled base hex
- If building a fort later: mage goes INSIDE the fortification

### Threat Response

| Threat | Response |
|--------|----------|
| Unknown units visible in base hex | FORBID entry immediately, assess faction |
| Hostile faction observed within 2 hexes | Stop all movement. Fortify base. Buy more goblins for guard. |
| Creature lair in adjacent hex | Do not enter until army is ready. Mark and avoid. |
| Silver drops below 500 | Stop non-essential expansion. CLAIM from reserve. Form taxer immediately. |
| Mage health threatened | Mage retreats to inner hex, guard holds outer position. |

## 8. Decision Rules

### Exploration and Settlement

**If scouts find a large city (3000+ pop) within 3 hexes:**
→ Move Seraphel there. Cities have cheap leaders for mage recruitment and high tax bases. This is the ideal base.

**If scouts find only small villages and wilderness:**
→ Stay at current swamp (30,8) in Taso. The 1502-goblin swamp has enough for a basic tax base. Send scouts further.

**If swamp (30,8) is threatened by a hostile unit:**
→ Do NOT fight. Move Seraphel SW to mountain (29,9) or SE to swamp (31,9) immediately. Never defend a hex with just the mage present.

### Silver Management

**If silver drops below 500:**
→ CLAIM from unclaimed reserve immediately. Do not let study stop. Add a new tax unit next turn.

**If unclaimed silver drops below 3,000:**
→ Stop all discretionary spending. Only fund mage upkeep and study. Prioritize establishing a tax unit before anything else.

**If silver exceeds 2,000 held:**
→ Recruit next mage or buy goblins for tax expansion. Do not hoard idle silver.

### Faction Upgrade

**Each turn, check:**
→ If current Mages count < Magic cap: do NOT rush to buy another mage yet unless Phase 2 conditions met.
→ If at Mage cap AND have silver: buy FACTION MAGIC upgrade before buying new mage.
→ Current state: Mages 1/2, Regions 0/10. Priority = Magic cap upgrade first turn it is affordable.

### Combat Situations

**If a creature group is in a region Seraphel needs:**
→ Wait. Build skeleton pool to 15+. Return with combat support. Do not risk Seraphel solo.

**If a hostile faction unit is in the same hex:**
→ Seraphel is BEHIND + AVOID. The hostile unit attacks the front guard first. If no guard: move Seraphel immediately, even at cost of study turn.

**If skeleton pool drops below 10:**
→ Cast SUSK every available turn. No movement. No combat. Replenish first.

**If creature nest (lair/ruin) is observed nearby:**
→ Note the location. Do not approach until: skeleton pool 20+, fire mage ready, and a tactics or COMB 3 unit is present as leader.

### Alliance and Diplomacy

**If another faction is encountered as Neutral:**
→ Default Unfriendly to Creatures is already set. For human factions: observe 1–2 turns before declaring. Offer Friendly if they have no conflict with Covenant territory.

**If a powerful faction offers alliance:**
→ Accept if they are Neutral-or-better and not adjacent to our base. Powerful allies can help capture city guards. We contribute mage firepower; they contribute melee.

## 10. Weaknesses to Mitigate

Drawn from observed magic factions that failed or struggled:

| Weakness | Mitigation |
|----------|------------|
| Single mage assassinated by stealth unit (Faction 15: Darkness killed turn 7) | Keep mage with guard unit. Have Observer mage (OBSE 3+) in base hex. |
| Over-reliance on allies for military (Faction 5: collapsed when allies were unavailable) | Build independent skeleton army before needing combat. |
| Mage cap hit, cannot buy new mages (Faction 33: stuck at 10 regions until turn 16) | Upgrade FACTION MAGIC proactively, not reactively. |
| Guard units can't GUARD because COMB 0 (Faction 5: lost a full turn) | Always study COMB before issuing GUARD orders. |
| Lost all mages in one battle (Faction 50: eliminated turn 17 after overextending) | Never send mages into an uncertain fight. Skeletons go first, mages stay BEHIND. |
| No fortification, mages can't study above level 2 (Faction 25: hit this wall turn 15) | Plan fort construction starting Phase 2. Build tower in base hex. |
| Undead army sits idle, never deployed (Faction 15: skeletons unused for 20 turns) | Set a minimum threshold for deployment. At 20+ skeletons, hunt the nearest monster lair. |
| Scouts lost to monsters in untested hexes (multiple factions) | Never send the only mage to scout. Buy a 1-goblin scout first. |

## Quick Reference: Turn Checklist

Every turn, before submitting orders:

1. **FACTION** order — upgrade if possible
2. **CLAIM** — take what you need for study + upkeep; check unclaimed balance
3. **Mage study/cast** — Seraphel studies or casts per current phase priority
4. **Teaching** — Seraphel teaches prerequisites to new mages if any
5. **Guard units** — verify each tax unit is on GUARD + TAX with COMB 1 confirmed
6. **Scouts** — check reports, move if they found something actionable
7. **New mage** — if silver and cap allows, buy next mage and assign study
8. **Skeleton carrier** — ensure skeletons are in carrier unit, not stranded
9. **Safety check** — no mage is in an unguarded hex without AVOID + BEHIND set

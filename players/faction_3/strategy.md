# Roma Aeterna — Strategic Framework
**Faction 3 | Martial 1 / Magic 1 | Military Empire**

---

## 1. Strategic Pillars

**Pillar 1: Tax first, fight second.**
Do not spend silver on large armies before you have a stable tax base. The war machine is funded by taxes. A Legatus with 5 centaurs cannot sustain itself without income — every early decision filters through "does this help establish taxes sooner?"

**Pillar 2: The Legatus is a mobile asset, not a garrison.**
The Legatus (141) has GATE 1. Use it. A mage pinned to one hex is wasted. Use gate jumps to explore, find a population center, recruit new mages, and position for the PHEN income path. Never leave the Legatus stuck on foot in the wilderness.

**Pillar 3: Economy before magic, magic before army.**
Priority order: establish tax income → unlock Phantasmal Entertainment (PHEN) → build combat-ready legions. PHEN generates ~500 silver/turn per mage at zero additional cost. Three mages casting PHEN = 1,500/turn — more than a 20-hex tax empire without the maintenance costs.

**Pillar 4: The peninsula is a trap — go north or northwest.**
The geography is ocean to E and S. All expansion is N/NW through forest (9,9) and toward Bosse [town] (8,10). Do not wait for sea access — claim land corridors aggressively.

**Pillar 5: Always stay at faction upgrade pace.**
Upgrade FACTION MAGIC or MARTIAL every time a new mage or combat slot is needed. Never be at the cap when you want to recruit. The upgrade cost is trivial compared to losing a turn waiting.

**Pillar 6: Overwhelming force, never solo.**
Do not attack a City Guard alone. Always have a tactics leader, multiple fire mages, and a melee stack before assaulting a guarded city. A single defeat can eliminate your entire mage pool. Seek allies for major assaults.

## 2. Phase Framework

### Phase 1: Foundation
**Condition:** Until first 3+ regions are being taxed and producing at least 600 silver/turn.

**Focus:**
- Legatus gate-jumps to find a large population center (city or town with 5,000+ peasants) suitable as a mage hub
- Recruit centaur or local-race tax units from Cehurst/Bosse areas while moving north
- Deploy tax guards in Cehurst hex (9,11) immediately — 1609 centaurs, $740 tax available
- Send scouts N and NW to map adjacent hexes
- Legatus begins PHEN prerequisite path (see Skill Priorities)

**Success looks like:** 3 regions taxed, 600+ silver/turn income, Legatus stationed at a city with 5,000+ population.

**Triggers next phase:** Tax income established, second mage recruited, Legatus has ILLU 1 ready to study PHEN.

---

### Phase 2: Expansion
**Condition:** Until a city or major town is secured and taxed, and PHEN is operational.

**Focus:**
- Legatus casts PHEN at population center, generating 400–600 silver/turn
- Second mage recruited, begins FORC→FIRE→PATT→ILLU→PHEN path
- Tax units deployed into all reachable forest and plain hexes to the north
- One tactics leader in training (TACT 1→5)
- Centaur combat units armed with swords from city spoils after first guard assault

**Success looks like:** Two mages casting PHEN (~900–1,000/turn), 8+ regions taxed (2,000+ silver/turn combined), tactics leader at TACT 2+.

**Triggers next phase:** City captured, 10+ regions, silver income stable above 3,000/turn.

---

### Phase 3: Empire
**Condition:** Ongoing — city secured, multiple mages, growing army.

**Focus:**
- Third mage recruited, all three eventually casting PHEN (1,400–1,500/turn from magic alone)
- Dedicated specialists trained: TACT 5, STEA 3+, OBSE 3+, QUAM 1
- Expand tax network to 20+ regions
- Build self-sufficient combat force (2 mages with FIRE 2 + 30+ centaurs with swords + TACT leader) capable of clearing 80-sword city guards without allies
- Begin artifact lore or enchantment path on primary mage

**Success looks like:** 5,000+ silver/turn, 20+ regions, independent military capable without allies.

## 3. Unit Archetypes

### Legatus (mage-commander)
- Role: Gate-jump explorer, PHEN income caster, combat fireball in battles
- Setup: BEHIND 1, AVOID 1, combat spell FIRE, NOAID 0
- Skill path: FORC→FIRE→PATT→ILLU→PHEN, then specialize (GATE 2, FARS 2, or ARTI)
- Always carries enough silver for maintenance; WITHDRAW meals to reduce maintenance cost
- Never in front of melee — always positioned behind

### Combat Legion
- Role: Main battle force, city guard assault, territory control
- Composition: 20–50 centaurs with swords, combat 1–2, on a tactics leader's stack
- Deployment: Attack city guards alongside mage fireball support; guard high-value towns post-capture
- Upgrade path: Add plate armor, then mithril weapons when production allows

### Tax Garrison
- Role: Claim regions, collect taxes, hold territory
- Composition: 5–10 centaurs (cheap at 68 silver in Cehurst) with combat 1, GUARD 1, @TAX
- Critical rule: Units MUST study combat 1 BEFORE issuing GUARD. Use TURN block: buy centaur → study COMB → TURN → GUARD 1 → @TAX → ENDTURN
- Minimum viable: 3 centaurs generates ~150 silver/turn; 10 centaurs ~500/turn
- One garrison unit per hex; do not stack multiple tax units in same hex unless tax base is very large (5,000+ population)

### Scout
- Role: Map regions, identify gates, find enemy, detect city guards
- Composition: 1 centaur (fast mover, 4 hexes/turn mounted) or lizardman (ocean coast scouting)
- Behavior: AVOID 1, SPOILS NONE, behind flag; never fight
- Critical rule: Never leave a lone scout in a hex that contains enemy units or creatures — scouts die instantly

### Builder / Specialist
- Role: Construct towers and castles, produce resources, quartermaster logistics
- Initially not critical — prioritize taxes and magic over production
- Build first tower (100 stone) near the mage hub once a builder has BUIL 2
- Quartermaster (QUAM) becomes essential from Phase 3 onward for supply chain management

## 4. Skill Development Priorities

**Order of priority with reasoning:**

1. **FORC 1** (already have) → enables FIRE 2 and is prerequisite for PHEN
2. **PATT 1** (already have) → prerequisite for PHEN
3. **ILLU 1** → only missing PHEN prerequisite; study this immediately upon reaching a city
4. **PHEN 1** → core income engine; every month delayed is ~500 silver lost
5. **COMB 3** (already have on Legatus) → sufficient for combat mage role
6. **FIRE 2** → doubles fireball output in battle (essential before any city guard attack)
7. **FORC 2** → required to access FIRE 2 (prerequisite chain)
8. **PATT 2** → increases PHEN income; study when convenient
9. **TACT 1–5** (on dedicated leader, not Legatus) → provides +1 per level attack bonus; TACT 3+ is decisive in battles
10. **GATE 2** (on Legatus, Phase 3) → enables teleportation
11. **FARS 2** (utility) → remote scouting; study when Legatus is not needed for PHEN
12. **ARTI 1+** (late game) → artifact creation for major power multipliers

**Rule:** ILLU → PHEN is the single most important skill unlock. Every other study takes second place until PHEN is casting on the primary mage.

**New mages path (when recruited):** FORC 1 → FIRE 1 → PATT 1 → ILLU 1 → PHEN. Teach where possible — having the Legatus teach speeds this to roughly half the time.

## 5. Economic Engine

### Priority order: TAX > PHEN > Work

**TAX** is the most scalable income. A 10-centaur guard unit collects ~500 silver/turn with 100 silver maintenance = 5:1 ROI. Scale this to 15+ hexes for 5,000+ silver/turn baseline by Phase 3.

**PHEN** (Phantasmal Entertainment) is the most capital-efficient single income action. Each mage casts it every turn in a large city (10,000+ population = ~600 silver/cast; 5,000 population = ~450 silver/cast). Three mages casting = 1,500/turn with no additional unit cost. This is effectively free income once the mage exists.

**Work** (unskilled labor) produces ~$12/person/turn in forest regions. Use only for new units waiting to reach their deployment region or when nothing better is available.

**Starting silver management:**
- Unclaimed: ~8,750 (report turn 1 shows 9,750 but 1,000 was spent on study)
- Reserve minimum 3,000 silver as operating capital for emergency recruitment and maintenance
- Spend aggressively on tax units in first 4 turns — each centaur tax unit pays back in 3–4 turns

**Income growth targets:**
- Phase 1 end: 600+ silver/turn from tax
- Phase 2 end: 2,500+ silver/turn (tax + 1 PHEN mage)
- Phase 3: 5,000+ silver/turn (tax + 3 PHEN mages)

## 6. Military Doctrine

### When to attack:
- City guards: Only with TACT 2+ leader, 2+ mages with FIRE 2, and 20+ melee fighters with swords. Never attack alone.
- Monster/creature blocking a hex: Attack if you have 10+ combat 1 fighters + any mage. Most creatures fall to 10 fighters with mage support.
- Enemy scouts/lone units: Attack immediately with nearest combat-ready unit to prevent intelligence gathering.
- Enemy faction combat units: Do not attack until superior force is confirmed (at least 2:1 numerical advantage + mage support).

### When to wait:
- If city guard has 80+ leaders with swords: Wait until FIRE 2 mages are available
- If no tactics leader present: Wait — tactics provides decisive advantage in round 1
- If silver reserve is below 2,000: Wait — replace lost units requires capital
- If allied support is available: Always wait for ally coordination before a city assault

### Minimum force requirements before attacking a City Guard:
- 1 tactics leader (TACT 2+) in the stack
- 2 mages with FIRE 2 casting fireballs (kills 20–30 guards per mage per round)
- 20+ melee fighters with swords
- OR equivalent ally force to supplement (one additional FIRE 2 mage = 1 ally mage = ~25 kills)

### Alliance vs. solo:
- Phase 1–2: Always seek allies for city assaults; offer mage fireball support in exchange for territory
- Phase 3+: Build independent assault capability but still prefer coordinated attacks
- Never join an ally attack unless mages are BEHIND and you understand the battle composition — mage loss is catastrophic

## 7. Decision Rules

**If silver drops below 2,000:**
→ Immediately stop buying new units. Prioritize maintaining existing tax units (no unit dies from starvation). Legatus casts PHEN this turn regardless of study plan. Reassess spending next turn.

**If hostile faction is detected in adjacent hex:**
→ Set default attitude to Unfriendly if not already done. Move a combat-ready centaur guard into the disputed hex. Legatus moves to a fortified structure if possible. Do NOT leave any lone units unprotected in border regions.

**If City Guard is present in target region:**
→ Do not attempt to TAX that region. Surround adjacent hexes with tax units to generate income while planning assault. Assemble the assault force (see Military Doctrine minimums) before attacking.

**If ally offers joint attack:**
→ Accept IF: your mages are in range (or reachable by gate), you have at least FIRE 1 on Legatus, and you will share in the spoils/territory. Decline if your mages have no attack contribution or if the battle is clearly unwinnable.

**If creature blocks expansion hex:**
→ If 3 or fewer creatures: send 10+ centaurs with combat 1 to clear it. If more than 3 or unknown strength: scout with avoid unit first, then commit a full combat stack. Never send lone scouts into creature-heavy hexes.

**If Faction reaches Mage cap:**
→ Immediately upgrade FACTION MAGIC before recruiting next mage. Cost: 4,000 silver for Magic 2 (which allows 3 mages + 4 apprentices at Martial 1). This is always worth it once you can afford it.

**If a tax unit is destroyed:**
→ Recruit replacement immediately from nearest market. Do not let a region sit unguarded for more than 1 turn — enemy scouts will claim it.

**If the Legatus is in a dangerous hex (enemy nearby, no garrison):**
→ Use CAST GATE RANDOM to escape — a gate jump is always available if Gate 54 is reachable, or if GATE 2 is learned. Do not MOVE on foot if enemies are adjacent.

## 8. Faction Upgrade Priority

Always spend Martial/Magic points. Never save upgrade points.

**Suggested upgrade path:**
- Magic 2 (costs silver): First upgrade — enables 3 mages + 4 apprentices. Do this when second mage is being recruited (Phase 2). At Martial 1, Magic 2: allows 1 + 1 + 1 = 3 mages at Magic 2.
- Martial 2: Second upgrade — enables more combat unit slots. Do this in Phase 3 when preparing a dedicated combat army.
- Magic 3: For a fourth mage (Phase 3 deep expansion). Each additional PHEN mage pays back the upgrade cost in ~8–10 turns.

**Rule:** Upgrade magic before buying the new mage, not after. The cap blocks recruitment — do not waste a turn.

**How to balance:** Martial vs. Magic upgrades are both useful. Roma Aeterna is a military empire, but the mage income (PHEN) is so lucrative that Magic 3 should precede Martial 3. Then balance back to Martial for army expansion.

## 9. Weaknesses to Mitigate

**W1: Lone units die.**
Faction 5 lost an ice dwarf scout to assassination in turn 3. Faction 20 had all northern scouts destroyed in one allied assault. Never deploy a single-man unit in a hex that contains hostile or unknown units. Minimum viable garrison: 5 fighters with combat 1.

**W2: GUARD prerequisite error.**
Units MUST reach combat 1 before GUARD works. Without it, the GUARD order silently fails. Use TURN blocks: buy unit → study COMB 1 (1 month) → TURN → @GUARD 1 → @TAX → ENDTURN. This is the single most common order error.

**W3: Over-relying on allies for all combat.**
Faction 5 (Sunshine) was eliminated after their core ally fell. If Roma Aeterna cannot clear a 80-sword city guard alone by Phase 3, it is strategically vulnerable. Invest in the independent assault capability.

**W4: Delayed PHEN acquisition.**
Faction 5 reached PHEN at turn 8 instead of turn 5–6 by studying SPIR first. The Legatus already has FORC 1 + PATT 1 + SPIR 1 + FIRE 1. Only ILLU 1 is needed. Study ILLU immediately when at a large city (turn 2 or 3 post-gate-jump). PHEN should be casting by turn 4–5.

**W5: Creature-infested hexes.**
Faction 5 lost two units to Lizard Men across the game and never solved it. Faction 10 was repelled by 8 demons. Before sending a tax unit into any non-city hex, check if the region has creatures. Any hex with 3+ creatures needs a dedicated combat unit to clear first.

**W6: Centaur maintenance in Cehurst is high.**
Centaurs cost 68 silver to buy and have moderate upkeep. While locally available, do not recruit more centaurs than needed for tax garrisons. Use cheaper races (goblins, gnolls ~$29–39) for secondary regions when available in later expansion.

**W7: Peninsula geography limits tax base.**
With ocean to E and S, the available regions are limited. By Phase 3, Roma Aeterna must control almost every available land hex N/NW to maximize income. Do not let ally factions claim land hexes that are in the viable expansion corridor.

## Quick Reference: Opening (Turns 2–5)

**Turn 2:**
- Legatus (141): CAST GATE RANDOM (explores map, finds population center), carry 5 centaurs for safety
- Form 1 tax unit (3–5 centaurs) at forest (9,11) — study COMB, then GUARD + TAX next turn
- Form 1 scout (1 centaur) → move North to (9,9) Nafirth

**Turn 3:**
- Legatus: if at city with 5,000+ pop → STUDY ILLU 1; else CAST GATE RANDOM again
- Tax unit from turn 2: if COMB 1 reached → GUARD 1, @TAX; else keep studying COMB
- Scout: report Nafirth region data; form second scout → NW to Bosse [town] (8,10)

**Turn 4:**
- Legatus: if ILLU done → STUDY PHEN 1; else finish ILLU
- Bosse scout: check town status, peasant count, available units for purchase
- Deploy second tax unit (buy centaurs locally or from Bosse)
- Form first combat stack (10 centaurs) at home base for garrison and future assault

**Turn 5:**
- Legatus: @CAST PHEN (first income ~400–600 silver!) + TEACH any newly recruited mage
- Tax income: should be coming in from 2–3 regions (300–600+ silver/turn)
- Begin recruiting second mage at city if budget allows (cost ~688 silver for leader)
- New mage starts: STUDY FORC 1 (or accept TEACH from Legatus)

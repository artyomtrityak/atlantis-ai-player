### Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong alliance diplomacy:** Declared 4 firm allies from turn 1 and maintained them the entire 20 turns. This provided military cover that the faction itself could not supply. Allies (Mantzikert 39 in particular) attacked cities, destroyed enemy informators, and absorbed enemy aggression.

2. **Consistent Earth Lore income:** Cast every single turn with zero lapses. At $27–31/turn it's modest but ensures the mage is always productive.

3. **Systematic mage development:** Three mages reaching force 2 / pattern 2 / spirit 2 by turn 20 — the prerequisites for powerful level-3 magic. The faction is clearly building toward high-level artifacts, enchantments, or summons.

4. **Teaching program:** Sharing pattern knowledge with allied mages creates network effects — by turn 17, 10+ allied mages have pattern skills and can pursue advanced magic.

5. **Efficient unclaimed silver usage:** Extracted nearly the entire starting pool (~5,900 silver) within 5 turns to build the initial army.

6. **Rapid response to new opportunities:** Bought new mages (Endymion turn 10, SkinnMacDana turn 11) immediately when capacity allowed via faction type change.

7. **Silver relay system:** The 2-scout bouncing courier between Utso and Onthead funded mage study reliably across turns 9–20.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Military overreach in turns 6–10:** Expanded to 5 regions rapidly but with 5–7 units of 5–10 unarmed gnolls/centaurs. No ranged capability (no longbows), no weapons, no armor. When a well-equipped enemy with longbows and tactics 3 arrived, the entire army was annihilated in one battle.

2. **No tactical protection against enemy tactics advantage:** Faction had tactics 1 (SkinnMacDana) vs enemy tactics 3 (Erlih). The 3-level difference means enemy attacks first with full +3 bonus — devastating against unarmored units.

3. **Unstable revenue base:** After the turn 10 defeat, tax income collapsed from ~$3,500/turn to the core 2–3 region income (~$1,000–1,500/turn). Recovery took 8+ turns and relied heavily on allies to hold regions.

4. **Mages studying outside buildings too long:** Warning appears turn 19: above level 2, magic study halved outside structures. The faction only enters a fort at Onthead by turn 20 — losing months of efficiency.

5. **Scout suicide advances:** Several scouts attacked enemy units alone (scout goblin at swamp 42,70 turn 7; scout 03 at plain 45,75 turn 10) and were destroyed with minimal damage to enemy. This wasted units.

6. **Recruitment strategy inconsistency:** Mixed gnolls, centaurs, goblins, orcs, humans, high elves — no coherent racial specialization. Centaurs move 4 hexes/month (riding), useful for fast expansion. Gnolls can train to combat 2 max but no special skills. Goblins are very cheap but weak. This was likely opportunistic buying rather than strategic.

7. **Recurring errors:** The `@steal` back-and-forth between Arkady (874) and scout 04 (1304) in mountain (43,81) — caught 8+ consecutive turns. This generated error messages and was never resolved.

8. **No production economy:** Zero weapons, armor, or trade goods produced by faction units. Completely dependent on buying from markets and allied production.

## Section 2: AI Agent Strategy (Based on This Playstyle)

### What the AI Would Keep

1. **The immediate 4-way ally declaration:** Declaring all known allies on turn 1 is correct and critical. The safety net of allied military presence is what allowed this faction to exist despite a catastrophic military defeat.

2. **Earth Lore as sustained income:** Cast every turn, never missed. The AI should replicate this — it's a reliable, always-on income source that scales slightly with mage skill.

3. **Three-mage development path:** Pursuing three mages toward force 2 / pattern 2 / spirit 2 / artifact lore is the right long-term investment. This enables the most powerful artifacts and summons in the game.

4. **Teaching allies:** Sharing foundation magic skills (pattern, force, spirit) with allies multiplies the faction's effective magical output at zero cost. An ally with earth lore casting in a rich region is pure income with no maintenance required from the faction.

### What the AI Would Change

1. **Do not overextend with unarmed troops (turns 5–10 mistake):** The faction built 5 regions of unarmed gnolls/centaurs with combat 1 only. Any well-equipped enemy (longbows, swords, tactics 3) destroys this entire force effortlessly. The AI should:

   - Invest in weapons (swords at minimum, longbows for ranged) for guard units
   - Track enemy military presence in regions before committing guard units

For fast expansion, centaurs are correct (4 hexes/month). For stationary guard/tax, gnolls or orcs with weapons are better investment.

3. **Working:** Used primarily during the first 2–3 turns for units that couldn't yet tax, or as backup income for scouts.

4. **Production:** Units were assigned to produce herbs, wood, iron, grain, livestock, and lassoes in specialized regions. This was expanded significantly from turns 9–15 as production units diversified with gnomes (herb, farming, lumber), humans (grain, mining, farming), and wood elves (lumber).

### Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**

1. **Alliance synergy:** The Surveyors exploited their 5-faction alliance masterfully. Battles were never fought alone — the combined firepower of 5 allied mages casting fire in round 1 was devastating. Unit 909 was just one of 5 fireball casters in the first major battle (turn 3), killing 6 in round 1, 5 in round 2, 7 in free round.

2. **Dual mage income:** PHEN mages (1571/1572) provided passive income growing from $614/turn (turn 7) to $1200/turn (turn 20), completely independent of region control. This cash funded ongoing magic investment.

3. **Systematic expansion via the "share" mechanic:** Each new tax region immediately spawned sub-units buying local races. The parent tax unit shared income to fund sub-unit purchases, creating self-bootstrapping expansion.

4. **Magic 3 capacity used efficiently:** The 4 mage slots were filled with distinct roles — 909 (combat/weather mage), 1571/1572 (PHEN income mages), 1573 (artifact crafter). By turn 20 all were producing meaningful value.

5. **Rapid region capping:** Reached 25/25 regions by turn 20 (month 20 of the game). This is impressive expansion speed.

6. **Race diversity:** Gnolls as primary combat/tax race (native), centaurs for mobility, high elves for ranged combat and high elven regions, humans for mining/building, goblins for scouting/combat in desert regions, wood elves for forests, gnomes for herb/lumberjack. Each race was placed in optimal terrain.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Weak independent military in early game:** For the first 10+ turns, the faction had no real combat capability outside of 909's fireball. The gnoll squads (unarmed) contributed virtually zero kills in alliance battles. The faction was entirely dependent on allies for military security.

2. **Silver ranking consistently low (30s–50s):** Despite having 25 regions, the faction's visible silver was always mid-to-low ranked, indicating significant reinvestment obligations and possibly over-reliance on claims in early turns.

3. **Scout losses:** Unit 5663 (lizardman) was killed by a Kraken in turn 18 while swimming through the ocean. Ocean expansion carried significant risk.

4. **Unit 7487 assassinated in turn 20:** A unit was assassinated in Dusdisoun, indicating the faction had units in regions where enemy stealthy units were active.

5. **Production region cap hit in turn 20:** Got the error "Faction can't produce in that many regions." This means the faction hit a game-engine limit on production, constraining further economic growth unless the limit was increased.

6. **TRANSPORT/logistics errors:** Turn 12 had a transport error (do not have 900 silver, transporting 861 instead). Some silver distribution was not quite synchronized.

7. **TAX errors early:** Turn 3 had "Unit 1578: TAX: Unit cannot tax" — suggesting a unit attempted tax without sufficient combat skill. This was a wasted action.

## Section 2: AI Agent Strategy (Based on This Playstyle)

### What the AI Would Keep

1. **The alliance framework:** Being Martial 2 / Magic 3, the faction is designed for magic support, not solo military dominance. The 5-faction alliance where each contributes fireballs is the correct approach. The AI should identify and coordinate with the ally block declared from turn 1.

2. **PHEN mages as economic backbone:** Two dedicated PHEN mages (1571/1572) provide compounding income that requires no region control. This is a very strong ROI — the $694 leader cost is recovered in 2–3 turns at PHEN level 1 ($307/turn × 2 = $614/turn). The AI should prioritize getting FORC + PATT prerequisites and transitioning to PHEN as fast as possible.

3. **Clear Skies on main mage:** Unit 909 casting Clear Skies every turn earns weather lore practice passively. This keeps the mage busy productively while studying other magic.

4. **Race-terrain matching:** Gnolls in tundra/plains/towns (native race bonus for buying), centaurs for mobility across all terrain, high elves in forests for tax, goblins in deserts, humans in plains/mountains, gnomes in forests/jungles. The AI should analyze the local race for sale in each new region and buy accordingly.

5. **Share-funded sub-units:** The "tax parent + share to buy child" pattern is highly efficient and should be replicated. Each established tax base funds its own troop expansion.

6. **Artifact creation:** By turn 18–20, the faction was creating flaming swords and windchimes. The AI should plan the magic tree paths (force + pattern + fire at high levels) needed for artifact creation in the mid-to-late game.

### What the AI Would Change

1. **Fix the early combat unit problem:** Turn 3 error "Unit 1578: TAX: Unit cannot tax" — gnolls with no combat skill cannot tax. Any unit intended to tax should have at least combat 1 before attempting to tax. The AI should ensure at minimum 1 level of combat on all tax units before issuing TAX.

2. **Reduce dependence on claims in early turns:** The faction claimed thousands of silver in turns 1–2 to bootstrap itself. This is correct and intended, but the AI should plan claim amounts precisely to avoid unclaimed balance going to waste. Turn 2 had 5,266 unclaimed silver — some was clearly not spent optimally.

3. **Faster PHEN transition:** 1571 spent turns 2–4 on force, pattern, illusion before reaching PHEN in turn 5. The minimum path to PHEN is force 1 + pattern 1, then PHEN can begin. The AI should have the two PHEN mages study force and pattern simultaneously (one each) and cross-teach each other, reaching PHEN by turn 3 instead of turn 5. This recovers ~$1,200 in foregone income.

4. **Address the "teaching incompatibility" errors:** In turn 3, 1573 attempted to teach allied mages who weren't studying the right skill. The AI should not issue TEACH orders without verifying what the target unit is actually studying. Use conditional TEACH only when coordinated.

5. **Don't sacrifice exploration units unnecessarily:** Unit 5663 (lizardman) was killed by a Kraken in ocean exploration (turn 18). The AI should check whether a region is ocean before sending a single unit through it, and use groups of swimmers if ocean crossing is necessary.

6. **Address the 13-region plateau (turns 11–13):** Three consecutive turns with no expansion suggests either scouts were not positioned correctly or the faction was waiting on income to fund new tax units. The AI should ensure at least 1–2 scout units are always moving toward new regions even during consolidation phases.

7. **Quartermaster establishment earlier:** The faction got its first quartermaster only in turn 18. The Martial 2 faction type grants 5 quartermaster slots (5), but only 1 was used by turn 20. Quartermasters dramatically help with logistics — the AI should aim to establish the first quartermaster by turn 10–12.

8. **Production specialization could start earlier:** By turn 8–9 the faction was doing herb, grain, wood, livestock production. The AI should evaluate which production is most profitable (lassoes at $84 each in turn 20 was very profitable) and specialize production units toward high-value goods.

### Recommended Opening (Turns 1–5)

  - Leader 1 (PHEN path): Claim ~$794, buy 1 leader ($694), study force (path: force 1 → pattern 1 → PHEN).
  - Leader 2 (PHEN path): Same.
  - Leader 3 (cross-teach): Claim ~$794, buy 1 leader, study pattern (to cross-teach with Leader 1/2 so both get force+pattern faster via TEACH).
  - Non-magic leader (utility): Claim ~$700+, buy 1 leader, study mining or observation for later utility.
  - Tax unit (combat gnolls): Claim ~$800, buy 15+ gnolls, study combat. This is the primary military/tax unit.
  - Support gnolls: Claim ~$300, buy 6 gnolls, study armorer (for armor production later).
  - Scout gnolls: Form 4–5 single-gnoll scouts, each moving a different direction from the starting town, to map adjacent regions immediately.

**Turn 2:**
- 909: Study clear skies (CLEA), add to cast each turn. Set @CAST CLEA so it casts automatically. Begin TEACHING force to the two PHEN-path leaders (to accelerate skill acquisition via 30-day practice bonus).
- Leader 1: Study force (being taught by 909 if possible, or self-study).
- Leader 2: Study pattern (cross-teach with Leader 3 to get pattern 1 faster).

**Turn 3:**

- Tax unit: TAX in the starting town. Ensure guard is set.
- Combat: Participate in alliance battle if the battle is initiated by allies. Do NOT attack unilaterally.

**Turn 5:**

- Military: Build a core combat unit of 15–20 gnolls with combat 1. Acquire swords (buy from markets or produce via armorer) and leather armor for this unit.

### Mid-Game Plan (Turns 6–15)

**Economic priorities:**
- Reach 15+ regions by turn 12 (the faction hit 13 and stalled for 3 turns — the AI should avoid this plateau).
- Ensure PHEN income is maintained and growing. Target $800+/turn from PHEN alone by turn 10.
- Establish at least 1 high-value production cluster: herb + lassoes (plant fiber → lassoes at $84+ each) is the most profitable identified in this game.
- Begin building a road network if possible (building skill required) to enable faster movement of cavalry between regions.

**Military priorities:**
- By turn 8–10, have at least 2 dedicated military stacks: one for offensive operations, one staying in key taxable regions as guards.
- Gnolls with combat 3 (via practice from battles) are the core. A unit of 20 gnolls with swords and leather armor is sufficient to clear most neutral guards and explorer-type enemies.
- Centaur cavalry with riding 2–3 can respond rapidly to threats.
- High elves in forest regions provide natural advantages (studying combat in forests gives racial bonuses in Atlantis).

**Magic priorities:**
- 909: Achieve force 3 + pattern 3 + weather lore 3 for long-range weather prediction. Then pursue earth lore or artifact lore for material production/creation.
- PHEN mages: Continue PHEN for income. At level 3+ PHEN, income is significant. Also study farsight (pattern 2 + observation 2 prerequisite) for remote region scouting without physical units.
- Artifact mage (1573 equivalent): Pursue force + pattern + fire combination to create flaming swords. A flaming sword significantly enhances a combat unit. One should be allocated to the main military stack by turn 15.

**Expansion priorities:**
- Prioritize regions with high populations (1500+ peasants = good tax base).
- Target town regions for purchase bonuses.
- Avoid being spread so thin that any single region can be easily captured by enemies.
- By turn 12, should have 18–20 regions controlled.

**Quartermaster:** Establish first quartermaster by turn 10. Use it to transport high-value resources (mithril, ironwood, rootstone) between production and crafting regions.

### Adaptations for Dynamic Conditions

**If the alliance breaks or an ally turns hostile:**
- The faction has no strong independent military for the first 10 turns. Immediately concentrate all combat units in 2–3 core regions and set Guard + Hold. Do not venture into exposed positions.
- Produce or buy swords and leather armor for the main gnoll combat stack immediately.
- Use the mage's fire combat to deter attackers — one mage with fire can kill 5–7 enemies per round, which is a strong deterrent against non-magic opponents.

**If a new hostile faction enters starting territory:**
- Use the "avoid + behind" positioning to keep valuable mages away from combat.
- Issue ATTACK orders only on units that are clearly inferior (single-unit scouts, etc.). Avoid attacking organized military stacks.
- Request ally assistance immediately — the multi-faction alliance model means military response should be coordinated.

**If expansion regions have strong native defenders (e.g., kobold packs, established city guards):**
- Wait for the alliance to provide combined combat before attacking. Never send single-unit scouts to regions with active monster packs (as seen in turn 6 where kobolds attacked Gyperboreya's units in Chayemner).
- If a region has a city guard, wait until the alliance has at least 3+ mages available for fire support, and ensure 40+ combat units in the attacking army.

**If the PHEN income mages are killed:**
- Replace immediately. The cost of rebuilding (1 turn to buy leader, 2 months to get force 1 + pattern 1, then PHEN) means 3 turns of lost income per mage. This is approximately $1,800–2,000 in lost PHEN income. Protect these units aggressively (avoid + behind + stay in guarded regions).

**If the faction hits the production region cap early:**
- Prioritize producing only the highest-value goods (lassoes, herbs, ironwood) rather than all types.
- Use the quartermaster to consolidate production to fewer regions.
- Consider adjusting the production skill mix — a unit producing lassoes ($84 each) is worth more than one producing grain ($10 each).

**If allies are losing ground in the shared town:**
- Contribute military units to the defense. The town (Leserstead) is the economic heart of the faction cluster — losing it would cascade negatively for all allies.
- If defending, set Guard + combat units at max in the town region.

**Diplomatic signals to watch:**
- The faction gave units to allied factions (2484 given to Thousand Masks, 2483 given to Thousand Masks, 2109 given to Thousand Masks). This "give unit" pattern is likely a coordinated operation where the ally takes over specific sub-units. The AI should be prepared to receive and use units from allies, as well as give units that the ally can operate more efficiently.
- Monitor for SEND orders from allied factions — this indicates resource sharing and should be reciprocated where possible.

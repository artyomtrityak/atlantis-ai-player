### Military Strategy

**No offensive military operations observed in turns 1–20.** The faction was purely defensive/avoidant.

**Key military facts:**
- All production and tax units used `avoid 1` and `behind` (standard defensive postures)
- Jeremy had COMB 3 (starting skill) but never fought
- Econquest units had COMB 1 (studied in turn 3) but were used only for taxing, not combat
- No weapons or armor were purchased for faction 45 units
- The faction observed but did not participate in a major battle: Turn 14 (Mar Yr2) saw Nonentity (67) + Wistful Thinking (27) and others assault and destroy the City Guard at Trasicy [city] — faction 45 was present in the city but was NOT part of the attack (it was done by other factions' mage coalition)
- Scout units used `spoils none` — they were not designed to pick up combat loot

**Econquest unit composition:** 5 lizardmen [LIZA] each, COMB 1. These are minimum-viable tax units. They have walking capacity but no weapons or armor. They would lose any serious fight.

**Assessment:** This is a purely economic faction build in the first 20 turns. They positioned units defensively (avoid, behind, revealing faction for diplomacy) and avoided all combat.

### Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**

1. **Exceptional mage starting skills:** Jeremy began with COMB 3, GATE 1, FIRE 1, FORC 1, PATT 1, SPIR 1 — an unusually strong foundational mage. The player recognized the gate lore path (PATT+SPIR+GATE) and committed to it immediately.

2. **Early diplomatic alignment:** Declared Fon (8), E.V.I.L. (68), and Cartographers (69) as Friendly by turn 4. This enabled coexistence in shared regions without conflict.

3. **Efficient tax unit design:** Econquest divisions of 5 lizardmen (COMB 1) were minimum-viable for taxing. Cheap to maintain ($50/unit/turn), difficult to kill without significant force, and highly mobile.

4. **Farsight scouting before committing:** Jeremy used farsight repeatedly to assess southern regions before physically moving there. This informed the gate-jump destination choices.

5. **Gate jump as strategic relocation:** Using GATE 2 RANDOM to teleport the entire tax cluster across the map is a powerful tactic that avoids long overland marches. The group jumped twice in consecutive turns (turns 16–17), covering vast distances.

6. **Ship for continued exploration:** The Raft (built turn 13) operated independently of the gate jump cluster, exploring ocean regions for fishing income and geographic intelligence.

7. **Production diversity:** Three specialized production units (Lumberjacks for wood/fish, Farmers for livestock, Architects for buildings/ships) provided some resource independence from taxation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lizardman choice for production:** Lizardmen cap at skill level 2 for farming, building, mining, lumberjack — the core economic skills. The Lumberjacks eventually pivoted to fishing (they can study fishing to level 5), but the Farmers were stuck at farming level 2. Orcs or hill dwarves would have been stronger for production roles.

2. **Zero military capability in 20 turns:** The faction had no combat force beyond Jeremy (with COMB 3 but no army) and the lightweight Econquest units. They were entirely dependent on other factions' protection and on avoiding conflict. Any hostile faction could have eliminated their tax network.

3. **No region control until very late:** "Regions: 0" status persisted until mid-game, meaning the faction had no secured territory, no defensive structures, and no population control.

4. **Maintenance cash flow problems:** The Lumberjacks, Farmers, and Ivwick Architects consistently needed to borrow silver from Econquest units for maintenance. This shows poor planning of cash distribution. The production units' maintenance exceeded their income in early turns.

5. **Gate jump is RANDOM:** The RANDOM gate jump is uncontrolled — the destination is unknown. Jumping twice in a row (turns 16–17) to land at progressively better regions was fortunate. There's no guarantee of landing in a good area.

6. **Trasicy city was captured by allies (turn 14):** The player benefited from allies taking the city guard at Trasicy — but faction 45 played no role. They were in a position of dependency on allied factions (Nonentity 67, Wistful Thinking 27) for city control.

7. **Magic path very narrow:** Jeremy invested 10+ turns purely in the gate lore path (PATT→SPIR→GATE). He then pivoted to EART and WOLF — utility magic. He never pursued combat magic (FIRE 1 was left at 1), weather magic, or summoning. A second mage was never created in 20 turns (faction had 1 mage / 2 allowed for entire game).

8. **Scout units poorly maintained:** Single-race scouts (1 gnome, 1 human, 1 lizardman) consistently had maintenance problems, often borrowing from nearby units. Scouts with races that work in their terrain (gnomes in jungle, humans in desert) could self-fund via @work, but many were placed in wrong terrain types.

## Section 2: AI Agent Strategy (Based on This Playstyle)

### What the AI Would Keep

1. **Gate lore as a strategic relocation tool.** The core concept — study PATT+SPIR+GATE to move the tax cluster across the map — is excellent. A well-positioned tax cluster can collect $900+/turn with just 4 units. The AI should pursue this path deliberately.

2. **Farsight scouting before committing troops.** CAST Farsight to examine distant regions before physically sending units there is efficient. Use it every turn while studying magic.

3. **Econquest-style tax units.** Small combat-skilled squads (5 fighters at COMB 1+) for taxing regions they do not own is efficient and scalable. The AI should maintain 3–5 such units.

4. **Multiple single-unit scouts for geographic mapping.** Deploy 1-man scouting units (using races native to the terrain to self-fund via WORK) immediately from turn 1. The faster the map is understood, the better the strategic decisions.

5. **Diplomatic declarations immediately.** Set Friendly to allied factions on turn 2 or 3. This enables coexistence and intelligence sharing.

6. **Ship for parallel exploration.** A Raft or Longship crewed by a production unit (fishing or working on ocean) enables geographic exploration independent of the mage's location. Continue this.

7. **Farsight practice passively.** Casting Farsight each turn while studying other skills accumulates passive PATT experience. Always set up a @CAST Farsight if Jeremy has the resources.

### What the AI Would Change

1. **Race selection for production units.** Lizardmen are a poor choice for farming and building (cap 2). The AI should recruit:
   - **Gnomes** for herb lore, quarrying, entertainment (cap 5) and crossbow
   - **Orcs** for mining, lumberjack, combat, building, shipbuilding (cap 5)
   - **Hill dwarves** for mining, building, smithing
   - **Wood elves** for lumberjack, herb lore, longbow
   Lizardmen should be used **only** for combat/tax units (since COMB is capped at 2, but they have swimming capacity — good for naval tax units).

2. **Start magic study on turn 1.** Jeremy failed his first study attempt (farsight) because of missing prereqs. The AI should verify prereqs before ordering STUDY. On turn 1, Jeremy should have claimed silver and begun studying PATT or FORC (which he already had at 1). Losing a turn to a failed STUDY is wasteful.

3. **Get a second mage as fast as possible.** Faction 45 had 2 mage slots but used only 1 for all 20 turns. Leaders are sold at cities ($627–$900 each). A second leader bought from Iywick by turn 3–4 could begin studying a second magic path. This doubles magical capability. The AI should purchase a second leader by turn 3 at latest.

4. **Plan gate jump destination.** Instead of RANDOM jumps, the AI should use farsight to scout specific gates (if gate lore is researched beyond 2, targeted gate jumps become possible). Alternatively, use RANDOM only after scouting the destination region via farsight. The faction was lucky both jumps landed in good towns.

5. **Build combat capability sooner.** Even 20–30 fighters with COMB 2+ would dramatically improve the faction's security. The AI should split a portion of income into building a military unit by turns 8–10. The Econquest combat units were fine for taxing but couldn't protect anything.

6. **Sell surplus resources.** The Lumberjacks produced 10 wood/turn early, the Ivwick Architects received this wood. But the Farmers' livestock (produced 10/turn) was mostly eaten by units for maintenance. The AI should sell excess livestock and wood at the nearest city to generate silver income rather than consume it internally.

7. **Fix maintenance cash flow.** The production units repeatedly borrowed silver from Econquest units because they had no local silver. The AI should either:
   - Assign dedicated silver-bearing units to each production region, OR
   - Have each production unit claim silver from the faction pool each turn based on their maintenance cost
   The turn-by-turn borrowing is not a bug but it's messy and can cause maintenance failures.

8. **Pursue a specific magic path beyond gate.** After achieving GATE 2, Jeremy pivoted to EART and WOLF — both interesting utility skills but with unclear tactical value in 20 turns. The AI should have a plan: e.g., FIRE 2 → FIRE 3 for fireball combat magic (powerful in sieges), or NECR for undead summoning, or ILLU for deception. Earth lore can produce horses and other utility.

9. **Don't let farsight go to waste.** Jeremy stopped casting Farsight after the gate jumps (because he was moving every turn). The AI should cast Farsight whenever stationary, even if just for passive PATT practice.

### Adaptations for Dynamic Conditions

**If hostile factions contest tax regions:**
- Move tax units away immediately (they are too weak to fight)
- Redirect Jeremy's gate jump toward the hostile region to assess the threat via farsight
- Build the combat squad earlier (turn 6 instead of 8) if hostility is detected
- Seek alliance with stronger factions (Nonentity 67, Wistful Thinking 27 pattern observed in game)

**If gate lore is blocked by prereqs or RNG:**
- Fall back to overland expansion with a caravan (Jeremy + tax cluster walking together)
- Study farsight more deeply to extend scouting range
- Build more scouts to cover more ground physically

**If allied factions capture cities:**
- Negotiate access (be Friendly, send ambassador units)
- Move into the city immediately after capture to buy units from there
- Don't assume alliance; maintain independent combat capability

**If a rich uncontested town is found:**
- Immediately send the tax cluster to it via overland or gate
- Station 3–4 tax units and begin taxing next turn
- Deploy scouts from there in all directions

**If second mage is achieved:**
- Split magic investment: one mage for gate/utility, one for combat
- Combat mage should study FIRE 2–3 (fireball) for city sieges, or NECR for army building
- Gate mage continues GATE 2 → GATE 3 for targeted (non-random) gate jumping

**Resource scarcity (low silver):**
- Immediately claim all available unclaimed silver
- Sell any stockpiled resources (wood, livestock, fish) at cities
- Put all non-specialist units on WORK orders
- Reduce unit count if necessary (dismiss scouts not generating information)

**If creatures or monsters threaten:**
- Use `avoid 1` on all non-combat units (faction 45 did this consistently)
- Declare Creatures (2) as Unfriendly (faction 45 always did this)
- Keep Jeremy away from dangerous regions unless he has a guard

**Finding gates early:**
- Prioritize scouting toward large towns and cities — gates are often located there
- Once a gate is found, send Jeremy there while continuing to build the tax base
- Don't gate jump until: (a) GATE 2 is achieved, (b) destination is scouted by farsight, (c) tax base can sustain itself without Jeremy's presence for 2–3 turns

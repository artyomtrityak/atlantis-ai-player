### Economic Strategy

Faction 50 built a multi-pillar economy by turn 4-5, centered on:

**1. Phantasmal Entertainment as the primary mage income source (turns 2+)**
- The One studied ILLU (turn 1), then PHEN (turn 2), and immediately began casting Phantasmal Entertainment each turn from turn 3 onward — raising ~$372 per cast at PHEN 1. By turn 9, with 4 mages all casting PHEN, income was ~$437 per cast per mage (4 × $437 = ~$1748/month from entertainment alone).
- This was the initial economic engine for the faction before tax income was established.

**2. Taxation as the backbone (turns 3–17)**
- Two large combat units were created turn 2/3 as tax collectors:
  - "Siaghhath Centaurs" (1537): 34–39 centaurs, taxing plain (16,58) — $1700–$1760/turn
  - "Siaghhath Gnolls" (2079): 35–40 gnolls, taxing plain (17,59) — $1750–$1870/turn
- Both units had COMB 1 and were `guard 1` (on guard) in their regions
- Turn 3 (report 4): Both units collected tax + fought and killed lone enemy units (Brother 2162, Bonker 1310) that tried to guard those hexes
- By turn 7 (after city guard assault), a major tax unit "Mesengoa Guards" (3738) was stationed at the town: $3600–$3750/turn, occasionally supplemented by pillage
- Additional small scouts in surrounding regions collected $50/turn each (6–8 scouts × $50 = $300–$400)

**3. Pillage as opportunistic income**
- Turn 5/6: scout (2078) pillaged plain (15,59) for $2706
- Turn 7: Evil Centaurs (3736) pillaged plain (14,58) for $1646
- Turn 11: Siaghhath Centaurs pillaged (16,58) for $3522; Siaghhath Gnolls pillaged (17,59) for $3752
- Turn 12: Evil Centaurs pillaged jungle (14,54) for $1334
- Pillage was used to drain regions before moving the main tax force elsewhere or when regions became contested

**4. Horse production (turns 4–17)**
- A unit "workers" (2776) with 10 gnolls trained in HORS (Horse Training) produced 10 horses/turn from plain (17,59)
- These horses were distributed to mages (for mounted mobility), "Brutal Raiders" unit (3075), and sold/used for riding combat bonus
- By turn 9, mages had horses (The One, Two, Three, Four, Five all had horse [HORS])

**5. Resource production (turns 8–17)**
- Orc lumberjacks (unit 4034, 10 orcs) trained in LUMB in jungle (15,55) Tinscillar — producing wood
- Orc shipbuilders (unit 4035, 5 orcs) trained in SHIP
- Gnoll hunters (unit 3741) trained in HUNT for fur production
- Lizardmen units scouted further regions (turns 15–16, unit 6029/6030 scouts)

### Military Strategy

**Phase 1: Establishing tax dominance (turns 1–4)**
- Immediately formed large stacks of centaurs and gnolls as tax/guard units
- Bought 33 centaurs at turn 2 (for 1537), 35 gnolls (for 2079)
- These units had COMB 1, were placed on guard in target hexes
- Attacked and killed blocking units (Gaggle faction's lone guards) in turns 3–4

**Phase 4: Aggressive expansion into contested zones (turns 14–16)**

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
1. **Fast economic ramp:** PHEN + multi-location taxation + pillage generated enormous income by turn 7–10
2. **Multi-mage coordination:** 4 mages with FIRE spells + TACT 1 made a devastating opening salvo; killed 80 city guards in one round
3. **Scout network:** Extensive centaur-on-mount scouts covered wide geography quickly (4 hexes/month vs walking 2)
4. **Flexible race composition:** Centaurs, gnolls, humans, high elves each used for their specialized skills (centaurs for riding, gnolls for combat/hunting, humans for building, orcs for lumberjack)
5. **Teaching efficiency:** The One's TEACH command accelerated 5–9 students per turn
6. **Alliance management:** Greywolf (33) was friendly throughout and fought alongside against city guard

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Overextension into contested zones:** The faction pushed into Blecishchan/desert region where Copy & Bara Union had an entrenched, large force with specialist anti-cavalry units (camel "terrify horses") and a mage with Energy Shield
2. **No anti-energy-shield capability:** When Copy & Bara Union's clerk (4826) cast Energy Shield, the faction's FIRE spells (magic energy attack) were blocked — The Five (3067) fired 0 of 0 times, other mages reduced effectiveness
3. **Loss of core mages to combat:** Placing all 5 mages into a single attacking battle was catastrophic; no reserve or home guard
4. **Underweaponed melee force:** Brutal Raiders had 36 swords for 25 gnolls+11 humans+20 horses — powerful, but the 74 goblins (Honmio Goblins) had no weapons/armor and were not effective
5. **Delayed region consolidation:** Despite massive silver income (rank 3 globally at some turns), region count stayed low (max 10, then dropped to 2) — possibly due to aggressive pillaging of own tax regions reducing peasant growth
6. **Pillage of home regions (turn 11):** Pillaging (16,58) and (17,59) — the primary tax regions — for ~$7000 one-time gain was shortsighted, as it reduced peasant population and thus long-term tax potential
7. **No defensive force at home:** While the combat force was away fighting Copy & Bara Union, there was no garrison remaining — a rival attacking from the north could have found the home territory undefended
8. **PHEN income not reinvested in mage defenses:** The faction earned $1748+/turn from PHEN but never developed defensive spells as primary for The One (FSHI, SSHI would help survive counterattacks)

## Section 2: AI Agent Strategy (Based on This Playstyle)

### What the AI Would Keep

1. **Phantasmal Entertainment as early income engine:** PHEN is highly efficient — $372–$445 per cast with just ILLU 1 and PHEN 1 prerequisites. The One's turn 3 PHEN cast paid for itself many times over. AI should replicate this immediately.

2. **Dual tax-unit model in first 2 turns:** Creating Siaghhath Centaurs (34 centaurs) and Siaghhath Gnolls (35 gnolls) as dedicated tax/guard units with COMB 1 was excellent. The faction funded these units through claims from unclaimed silver, established tax bases before turn 5.

3. **Centaur scout network:** Centaurs (50 weight, 4-hex/month with riding) are superb scouts. Faction 50's pattern of forming 1-centaur scouts each turn in each new hex, then working or scouting adjacent hexes, gave rapid map coverage. The scout unit template (`_s_cent` with `guard 0, avoid 1, behind 1, reveal faction, hold 1, noaid 1`) is well-designed.

4. **Teaching from The One:** The One's TEACH order to multiple mages simultaneously is excellent force multiplication. AI should always include TEACH in The One's orders when others are present studying the same skills.

5. **Faction rebalance to Magic 4:** Shifting to Martial 1/Magic 4 on turn 7 (after needing the combat power to defeat city guard) increased the mage cap. AI should plan this rebalance at a natural milestone — ideally after the initial military objective is achieved.

6. **Multi-race specialization:** The faction's use of orcs for lumberjack/ship, gnolls for combat/hunting, high elves for fishing/longbow, humans for farming, centaurs for riding/scouting was intelligent. AI should buy races whose skill caps match the needed role.

7. **Horse production for mount distribution:** The gnoll horse-trainers producing 10 horses/month, then distributing to mages (giving +1/+2 combat bonus and mobility) was valuable. AI should establish this by turn 5.

8. **Aggressive early city guard removal:** The turn 7 assault on Mesengoa's city guard (using combined mage fireballs + melee) unlocked $6000+/turn of taxable wealth. AI should plan this attack as a primary early-game objective.

### What the AI Would Change

1. **Never put all mages into a single attack without reserves:** Faction 50 lost all 5 mages in one battle. AI should keep at least 1–2 mages (ideally The One) safe at home unless victory is near-certain. If attacking Copy & Bara Union with 5 mages, leave home guards with stealth detection or a mage to warn.

2. **Research Energy Shield BEFORE attacking energy-shielded enemies:** The faction encountered Copy & Bara Union's Energy Shield and had no counter. The Three (1539) had ESHI 1 as early as turn 8 — but apparently wasn't using it effectively or the opposing shield was higher level. AI should: (a) detect enemy magic before committing, (b) have mages who can cast counter-shields. Specifically, The Five should have ESHI or FSHI by the time major battles occur.

3. **Invest in melee quality, not just quantity:** The 74 goblin "Honmio Goblins" unit had zero weapons and contributed almost nothing. AI should buy weapons (swords, pikes) before purchasing troops, or use gnolls which have better natural combat potential. Spend fewer silver on unarmed cannon-fodder.

4. **Do not pillage own primary tax regions (turn 11 mistake):** Pillaging (16,58) and (17,59) for $7000 one-time gain while simultaneously using those regions for $3500+/month tax was counterproductive. The 50% peasant reduction from pillage costs more in long-term tax income. AI should only pillage enemy regions or truly expendable regions before abandoning them.

5. **Maintain region count above 5:** The faction often operated at 2–4 regions despite having economic capacity for more. More regions = more tax income, more geographic coverage, more warning of threats. AI should station tax guards in all controllable hexes.

6. **Use ESHI as a defensive spell on The Three earlier:** The Three studied ESHI on turn 8. AI should set ESHI as The Three's combat spell from that point onward (it was not done consistently — orders show `combat ESHI` being set in turn 10 orders, but battle results from turn 7 show energy shield cast by allied Greywolf unit "Its Wilfred" not the player's own mages).

7. **Scout ahead of the combat force by 2–3 turns:** Faction 50 sent scouts into Blecishchan but the combat force followed blind. The loss came because the enemy was much stronger than expected. AI should spend 2 turns observing enemy composition before attacking. The Observation mages (The Seven, The Eight) should be tasked to shadow the enemy, using OBSE for spotting.

8. **Delay the desert offensive until mages have FIRE 2+:** At the time of the catastrophic battle, The One had FORC 2/FIRE 1 base, possibly FIRE 2 via teaching. The attack on Copy & Bara Union would have been more survivable with FIRE 2 giving more fireballs per round. AI should push all mages to FIRE 2 before major offensive operations.

9. **Stealth leader "Killer" (3414) was underdeveloped:** This leader had STEA 1, RIDI 1, COMB 1 by turn 15 — very weak. AI should either invest in developing a real assassin/thief (STEA 3–5, OBSE 2+) or skip STEA entirely and use the leader slot for another mage.

10. **Ally management:** The Greywolf (33) alliance was clearly valuable (they fought together at turn 7). AI should formalize alliances with reciprocal ALLY declarations, not just FRIENDLY. Sharing map data and coordinating would have improved both factions' outcomes.

### Adaptations for Dynamic Conditions

**If an early neighbor faction is aggressive:**
- Immediately build up tax units to 40+ units each (centaurs preferred — better combat)
- Attack city guard early (turn 6 if needed) to free the town from guard for maximum tax
- Declare the neighbor Unfriendly, set The Three to ESHI as combat spell, plan 4-mage coordinated defense
- Do not let them occupy neighboring plains unchallenged — even 1 centaur guard deters casual pillagers

**If the best tax regions are all occupied by enemies/guards:**
- Scout for unoccupied regions further away; use centaur scouts 2–3 hexes out
- Consider taxing lower-value regions ($800+) rather than trying to fight for premium regions early
- Use gold from PHEN + Times reward to fund a mobile raider group capable of clearing regions

**If a Kiiran-type faction (aggressive, cavalry, TACT 2) appears in your scouting zone:**
- Do not engage with scouts — they will be killed
- Declare Unfriendly, build ESHI on The Three, wait for them to attack into your strong zone
- Counterattack with full combined force only after observing their composition

**If energy-shielded enemies are encountered:**
- The Three (ESHI) can contest an enemy shield if same level — study more ESHI
- Alternatively, invest in spirit/force shield (FSHI, SSHI) — these counter different attack types
- Fireballs are magic energy attacks — they are fully blocked by equal ESHI. Switch to melee-dominant force or use non-energy magic (lightning, earthquake) if available
- The correct response is NOT to throw more fireballs but to bring overwhelming melee after mages die

**If silver runs low:**
- PHEN income scales with PHEN level — study PHEN 2 ($800/cast) as a priority
- Add more mages casting PHEN (each mage adds $400–800/turn)
- Short-term: use the Times reward ($200/turn) to cover maintenance
- Do not buy unarmed troop masses (like 74 goblins) — they drain silver with no combat value

**If friendly faction nearby (like Greywolf 33):**
- Declare them ALLY (not just Friendly) — this enables full combat cooperation
- Coordinate attacks: their mages + your mages = devastating combined firepower
- Share map data via scout movements
- Give resources if they are short — they will reciprocate in battles

**Signaling to avoid diplomatic incidents:**
- The `reveal faction` flag on scouts shows faction identity — neighboring factions will see "Brutals (50)" and can make contact
- Keep default attitude Neutral or adjust specific factions; avoid broad Unfriendly declarations that may antagonize potential allies
- Declare The Gaggle (22) or Copy & Bara Union (56) equivalents Unfriendly only after first hostile encounter

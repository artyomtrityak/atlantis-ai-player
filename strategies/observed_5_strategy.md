Faction: **Sunshine (5)**
Data range: Turns 1–20 (January Year 1 through September Year 2)

## Section 1: Observed Playstyle (Turns 2–20)

### Starting Conditions

**Faction type:** Martial 1 / Magic 1 at game start, quickly upgraded.  
**Starting units:**
- Unit 859: Leader, the mage/explorer. Starts in nexus with pattern 2, force 1, fire 1, combat 3, gate lore 1 skills already set. Combat spell set to fire. Uses `CAST GATE RANDOM` every turn to jump between gates.

- Unit 1209: 1 ice dwarf with horse (explores and scouts).

**Starting location cluster:** tundra (4,82) in Febefe, near cities E'egrad (5,79) and Meldorf (6,82) — both gnome cities. Native race in the region is ice dwarves.

**Starting silver:** ~10,129 unclaimed. Faction collects Times reward of 200 silver per turn.

**Diplomatic stance on turn 1:** Default Neutral. By turn 4 (May year 1), switched to Default Unfriendly with explicit allies declared:
- Ally: Fon (8), The Gaggle (22), Deathscarab (57)
- Friendly: squirrels (43), Trader Enclave (30), The Wandering Minstrels (70)

### Economic Strategy

**Core income streams:**

1. **Tax income from a Heda cluster** (established by turn 5–6): Unit 1387 (10 goblins) taxes ~500 silver/turn from swamp (31,19) in Heda. By turn 8, six additional single-goblin units tax surrounding hexes (~50 silver/hex/turn). This cluster generates ~800+ silver/turn in taxes from ~7 hexes by turn 9.

2. **Phantasmal Entertainment (CAST PHEN)**: Unit 859 learns this spell by turn 8 (September year 1). By turn 9, all three mages cast PHEN, each raising ~469–524 silver/turn/mage. By turn 12 (January year 2) all three mages are casting PHEN simultaneously for ~1,400 silver total. This becomes the dominant income source by mid-game.

3. **Large orc labor force:** Unit 2195 (50 orcs) works at swamp (16,74) earning 675 silver/turn for many turns. This is a massive early income unit. The orc unit is raised by buying orcs with bulk silver from allies and working indefinitely.

4. **High-elf working units in forest towns:** Unit 1853 (10–15 high elves) earns 141–183 silver/turn in forest (14,82) in Ersatrork town.

5. **Times reward:** 200 silver/turn every turn, reliably.

6. **Rianmoss tax base:** After capturing the city guard in swamp (16,74) in Rianmoss (turn 10 with ally Fon), units 1983 and 2005 collect ~1,467–1,900 and ~703–1,150 silver respectively in taxes — a very large income.

The progression shows aggressive early spending (building units, studying magic), followed by stabilization, then compound growth from PHEN + tax income.

**Buying and recruiting patterns:**

### Military Strategy

Unit 2195 (50 orcs) + Ally Fon (8) attacks City Guard (63) in swamp (16,74) in Rianmoss. Faction 8's mages (units 862, 1816, 1817, 1818) cast fireballs. Unit 2195 provides numerical melee support. Guard destroyed; spoils: 40 swords, 2000 silver. This was a pre-planned cooperative assault — Sunshine's 50-orc unit was clearly staged here for this purpose.

Unit 859 + Units 2058 and 2169 (all three mages) + massive Deathscarab (57) force attacks City Guard (10) — 120 leaders with 120 swords. Three Sunshine mages cast fireballs alongside Deathscarab mages. Two rounds, guard destroyed. This is faction 5's first major contested city capture with own mages.

**Mid-game: Let allies do combat, participate as support**  
Sunshine's mages join allied attacks on city guards throughout turns 16–20 (Deathscarab attacks city guards in plain (29,23), plain (32,26), swamp (32,22)). Sunshine units fight alongside but are not the primary military force.

**Guard units (personal tax defense):**

**Combat skills on line units:** Most guard/tax units study to combat 1, then observation 1 for stealth detection. No serious investment in elite infantry.

**Alliance dependency:** Sunshine relies heavily on Fon (8) and Deathscarab (57) for heavy military operations. Both allies have multiple mages with fireball. Sunshine's role is participation, not leadership, in joint battles.

### Expansion Pattern

**Phase 1 (Turns 1–4): Scatter scouts, find territory**  
From nexus, unit 859 gate-jumps randomly to explore. Units 1208, 1209, 1792, 1793, 1794, 1795, 1796 are formed and sent in different directions with horses for 3–4 hex/turn mobility. They primarily sit at regions, observe, and don't claim territory (0 regions claimed turns 1–4).

Scouts land at:
- Febefe tundra cluster (near starting area)
- E'egrad and Meldorf cities
- Wune tundra (13,85) — gate hex
- Loli plains (15,87, 16,88)
- Saslo tundra (city Toydintburg)
- Sofpuctaph plains (55,83)
- Elgenio plains
- Heda/Idio swamp cluster (gate jump)
- Gawdylcre forest

**Phase 2 (Turns 4–7): Claim Heda cluster, find Scretibis**  
Unit 859 gate-jumps to swamp (31,19) in Heda. This becomes the primary military expansion zone. Seven goblin units are formed here and sent to all 6 adjacent hexes + further south. By turn 5, 7 units have GUARD+STUDY orders ready to tax adjacent hexes.

Unit 859 moves overland with a horse from Heda toward Scretibis (28,14) — a large goblin city — arriving by turn 6. This is the mage headquarters for the rest of the game (turns 6–20+).

**Phase 3 (Turns 7–12): Consolidate Heda + Rianmoss, expand south**  
Heda cluster generates ~800 silver/turn in taxes. Rianmoss (16,74) is secured with allied help (turn 10), becoming a very high tax zone (~3,000/turn). Faction regions: 0 → 1 (turn 4) → 8 (turn 9) → 9 (turn 10) → 8 (some lost to creatures) → stable 8–9.

**Phase 4 (Turns 12–20): Buy specialists, ally with Deathscarab for city-clearing**  
Deathscarab (57) systematically clears city guards from Feles plains. Sunshine participates in all battles with mages. Regions grow from 8 to 11 (turn 18), then 13 (turn 20). New Quartermaster roles appear.

**Geographic footprint:**
- Primary base: Scretibis (28,14) — mage hub, phantasmal entertainment
- Tax cluster: Heda swamps (30-33, 17-23) — 7 hexes
- Income node: Rianmoss swamp (16,74) — ~3,000 silver/turn taxes
- Scouts/observation: scattered across Febefe, Wune, Sofpuctaph, Loli, mountain regions

**Coverage by turn 20:** Spans a large swath from Gawdylcre/Fe'eind (mountain 14,72) in the north to Feles plains (29-32, 21-26) in the south-center, Filebom swamps (6,78) to the west, and Sofpuctaph plains (55,83) to the east — approximately 30+ hexes visible.

### Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**

1. **Exceptional early silver income:** Claimed silver + Times reward + orc working unit gave a massive starting advantage. The faction began with 10,000+ unclaimed silver and 200/turn reward.

2. **Mage setup is elegant:** Three fire mages at one city, all casting PHEN = 1,400–1,500 silver/turn from magic alone by turn 12. Each mage also has fire 1+ for combat fireball.

3. **Gate exploration:** Using CAST GATE RANDOM to jump the mage across the map in turns 1–4 efficiently located a good base (Scretibis in Heda swamps — large goblin city, adjacent to a rich Heda swamp cluster for taxing).

4. **Ally integration:** Deep alliance with Fon (8), Deathscarab (57), squirrels (43). Allies provide gift horses (centaur mounts), participate in joint battles, and coordinate territory acquisition. Sunshine contributes mage firepower to allied attacks and receives territory in return.

5. **Cheap tax expansion:** Using $29 goblins to guard and tax swamp hexes is highly efficient. 7 goblins covering 7 hexes generating ~800 silver/turn with near-zero upkeep.

6. **Specialist mage pipeline:** By turn 15, building mages toward STEA 5, RIDI 5, TACT 5, COMB 5, QUAM 5. This suggests preparation for high-level operations (assassinations, fast movement, tactical bonuses, artifacts, quartermaster supply chains).

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Creature vulnerability:** Unguarded single-man units are repeatedly attacked by monsters (Crocodiles in Heda, Lizard Men in Idio swamps). Units 1922, 2457 destroyed. Unit 1208 assassinated turn 3. The faction never fully solved the problem of lone scouts in creature-infested hexes.

2. **Delayed PHEN:** PHEN could have been acquired sooner. The mage spent turns 1–7 studying SPIR, gate jumping, and exploring before settling on ILLU + PHEN path. A direct path (FORC 1 → PATT 1 → ILLU 1 → PHEN) could have had PHEN by turn 5–6 instead of turn 8.

3. **Slow region expansion:** Regions hovered at 8–9 for turns 9–17 despite large silver reserves. The faction could have claimed more territory faster by deploying more guard units. The Heda cluster was the only real territorial base for a long time.

4. **Observation/stealth timing:** Scout units studied OBSE 1, then STEA 1 over 2–3 turns before becoming functional. This slow training sequence means scouts were not useful for many turns.

5. **Guard error on turn 5:** All 6 new goblin units failed to GUARD (error: "Must be combat ready") because they hadn't studied combat yet. The player forgot units need at least combat 1 to guard. This delayed taxing by one full turn.

6. **No independent military force:** All major military is ally-dependent. Sunshine has no standalone combat force capable of clearing a city guard without Fon or Deathscarab. This is a strategic vulnerability if allies turn hostile.

7. **High maintenance costs:** Unit 2195 (50 orcs) costs 500 silver/turn in maintenance. It generates 675/turn working but this ties up a large amount of capital in one high-risk unit.

## Section 2: AI Agent Strategy (Based on This Playstyle)

### What the AI Would Keep

1. **Gate-jump exploration in turns 1–3:** Using CAST GATE RANDOM is a brilliant free exploration tool. The mage can jump 1–2 times while also studying 1 month/turn. This quickly reveals geography and gates with zero movement cost.

2. **Three mages at one location casting PHEN:** Having all three mages at the same city casting Phantasmal Entertainment simultaneously is highly capital-efficient. The city provides the large population needed for maximum PHEN income. Keep mages co-located for teaching efficiency too.

3. **Goblin tax clusters:** Single goblins in swamp hexes are the most efficient tax-to-cost ratio. $29/goblin, $10/turn maintenance, $50/turn tax income = 5:1 ROI. Once combat 1 is reached, they can guard.

4. **Alliance-first combat:** Participating in allied city captures as support (providing mage firepower) rather than leading solo assaults is sound. Sunshine's mages contribute meaningfully while not bearing full risk.

5. **Orc working units:** 50 orcs in a high-wage region generates 675 silver/turn. The 500 silver/turn maintenance is still profitable. Scale this principle for early income.

6. **Specialist mage pipeline:** Dedicating separate leaders to TACT 5, STEA 5, RIDI 5, COMB 5, QUAM 5 creates long-term operational capability. The AI should start this pipeline earlier (turn 8–10 instead of turn 15).

### What the AI Would Change

1. **Speed up PHEN acquisition (target turn 5–6, not turn 8):**  
   Faction 5 spent turns studying SPIR 2 before pivoting to ILLU → PHEN. PHEN prerequisites are: FORC 1 + PATT 1 + ILLU 1. The mage already had FORC 1 and PATT 2 from the nexus. Only ILLU 1 was needed. If the player had headed to a city immediately (instead of gate-jumping 4 turns), ILLU + PHEN could have been studied in turns 2–3. This would have added ~2,000+ silver in PHEN income over the delay.

2. **Buy second and third mages earlier (target turn 5, not turn 7):**  
   Leaders are bought at cities. The mage was not at a city until turn 6. AI should plan the mage's gate-jump or travel path to reach a large city by turn 4 so it can buy two more leaders immediately. Each additional PHEN caster adds ~500 silver/turn.

3. **Address creature-infested hexes proactively:**  
   Lizard Men (304) in Idio swamps (32,20) killed two units across the game. AI should recognize this hazard earlier and either avoid these hexes or deploy a combat-capable guard unit to clear or protect the area. A single unit with combat 2 + sword can typically drive off 3 lizardmen.

4. **Guard prerequisite error avoidance:**  
   On turn 5, all 6 new goblin units failed to GUARD because they lacked combat 1. AI must verify: units need at least combat 1 (30 XP / $10 study) before GUARD. Plan: buy goblin → study combat 1 in month 1 → GUARD + TAX from month 2. The faction used TURN blocks for this but failed to apply them correctly.

5. **Claim regions more aggressively:**  
   Faction 5 sat at 8–9 regions for 9 turns. With silver in the tens of thousands, the AI should be deploying new guard units constantly. Each region claimed = better faction limits on mages, Quartermasters, and unit count.

6. **Develop an independent military force:**  
   Reliance on allies for all city-clearing is a vulnerability. By turn 15, the AI should have at least one combat 5 mage + a tactics 3 leader + 20–30 armed guards capable of clearing an 80-sword city guard unilaterally.

7. **Use lizardmen/centaurs as dedicated ocean/land scouts:**  
   Faction 5 used lizardman 1792 effectively — it swam between ocean hexes to scout coastal territory. AI should maintain 2 lizardman scouts (ocean coast) and 2 centaur scouts (land) at all times.

### Adaptations for Dynamic Conditions

**If an ally turns hostile:**
- Immediately move all mages to a fortified city (use ENTER if a tower or castle is available)
- Deploy observation units (OBSE 3+) to detect stealth units approaching
- Rapidly buy 20–30 armed guards (orcs with swords) around mage location
- Do not panic-move mages — let them stay put and fortify rather than flee into an unknown area

**If creature attacks are persistent:**
- Any hex with 3+ creature units requires a dedicated guard with combat 2+ and a weapon
- Lizardmen and Crocodiles commonly attack lone civilian units. Rule: never leave a WORK or STUDY unit alone in a non-city hex without a combat-ready companion
- Budget 1–2 combat goblins per tax cluster as "creature insurance"

**If silver income drops (e.g., ally takes key tax regions):**
- PHEN is the reliable fallback — mages can cast it anywhere near a large population center
- Immediately cut spending on unnecessary worker units
- Prioritize QUAM (Quartermaster) to reduce unit maintenance costs

**If the game has many active human factions nearby:**
- Invest in OBSE 3+ units in every owned region for stealth detection
- Invest in one STEA 5 unit for assassination of enemy mages or scout leaders
- Declare Unfriendly as default (Sunshine does this by turn 4) — correct call

**If magic cap hits 3 mages:**
- Faction limits can be upgraded with FACTION MAGIC or MARTIAL orders. If at magic cap, spend the upgrade silver to increase to 4 mages before buying more leaders.
- Faction 5 upgraded to Martial 3 + Magic 2 early (turn 4), enabling 3 mages and 3 apprentices. AI should always upgrade the faction limit immediately when approaching it.

**Opportunity recognition — city capture:**
- Always participate in allied city captures when mages are nearby. The silver/sword spoils are valuable. Being present = spoils share and territory claim.
- If a city guard is in a hex you want to tax, coordinate with an ally to clear it. Faction 5 never cleared a city guard solo — always with allies. This is tactically sound given the guard sizes (80–120 leaders with swords).
